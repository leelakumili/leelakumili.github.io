Birthday Countdown Game Pack

I built my daughter a birthday present instead of buying her one. Ten days, ten games, one unlocking each morning, ending with a clue hunt around the house to find her actual gift.

She was turning 10. I wanted her to feel the anticipation, not just get a thing. Along the way, I learned more about prompt discipline with GitHub Copilot than any work project taught me — turns out the same principle applies: give it the right context, get consistent output.

Visibility Engine

Engineers do extraordinary work that no one sees — not because the work wasn't good, but because it wasn't narrated. Visibility Engine turns your raw weekly notes into a 1:1 talking point, a brag document entry organized by goal bucket, and a portfolio bullet. Five minutes on Monday. Everything else accumulates.

I was spending review season trying to reconstruct three months of impact from memory. Half my highest-value work — unblocking teams, making the call no one wrote down, absorbing the friction so my engineers didn't have to — never showed up in a PR. I built this for myself first, then kept using it.

Waypoint

Waypoint is a production MCP server that gives AI agents a leader's view of the team: context, decisions, coaching prompts, and unblocking tools, all over a live HTTPS endpoint. You set the coordinates. The team flies.

The best engineering leads don't manage the flight. They file the flight plan and step back. Waypoint is that operating model built into something real: not a framework or a slide deck, a protocol server you can actually connect to.

About

The person behind the systems.

I'm Leela Kumili, Engineering Lead at Starbucks, where I work on the Rewards Platform: the distributed, cloud-native system behind one of the most-used loyalty programs in the world.

Beyond building systems, I spend a lot of time thinking about how to build the people around them. I run informal 1:1s with my full team and peers, mentor engineers at various stages of their careers, and have spent years studying what actually separates a great technical lead from a great engineering manager.

I'm also an AI Champion at Starbucks, an InfoQ Editor covering distributed systems and architecture, and a conference speaker. My writing and talks focus on what happens in production: the incidents, the trade-offs, the decisions that don't show up in tutorials.

I'm working toward my first Engineering Manager role in 2026. Everything I build and write is part of that journey, in public, with honesty, earning the seat.
